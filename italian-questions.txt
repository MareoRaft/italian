answered q's
------------

Difference btw "addosso" and "su".  When to use which? -- use "su" almost always
  * addosso = su corpo
  * addosso is only used for putting something on yourself, used very rarely
  * quell'uomo mi è venuto addosso
  * oggi ho una maglietta bianca addosso
  * oggi indosso (the verb form) una maglietta

ed vs e?  when to use?
  - "ed" means "and".  it replaces "e".
  - vowel, use ed
  - consonant, use e

word eccitato?  should use emozionato instead?
  - yes
  - use "emozionato" for excited
  - but you can use eccitato for animals if you want

if i say "il mio telefonino è spento", am I saying it's out of batteries, or broken, or permanently broken?
  - it's turned off
  - use "scarico" if you want to say charge is empty
