Food Vocabulary (top)

A typical italian meal is eaten in four courses.

1) Appetizers - L'antipasto
2) First Course - Il Primo (pasta/carbs)
3) Second Course - Il Secondo (meat/fish)
3) Side Dish - Il Contorno (ordered with second course)
4) Dessert - Il Dolce

Certain dishes belong to certain courses.

L'antipasto

  • prosciutto e melone-ham and cantaloupe

Verb Conjugator (top)

This conjugator will work for any regular verbs and a few irregular. It currently handles presente, passato, passato remoto, imperfetto, trapassato, trapassato remoto, futuro, and futuro anteriore tenses. You may input the tense in Italian or English, but the program is glitchy and some of the English tenses won't work. The conjugator can also handle reflexive and reciprocol verbs. Another good site for verb conjugation is www.verbix.com/languages/italian.shtml. I learned many new tenses from this site.
