Spanish top

Consecutive consonants in Spanish where the first of the two is "hard" will need to be replaced by doubled consonants in Italian using the SECOND of the pair.
Spanish -> Italian
ct -> tt (actual -> attuale)
pt -> tt (capturar -> catturare)
bt -> tt (obtuso -> ottuso)
dv -> vv (adverbo -> avverbo)
bv -> vv (obvio -> ovvio)
mn -> nn (himno -> inno)


Other doublings have different resolutions, and the rule is not as strict as the above.
dj -> gg
bj -> gg or bb
bl -> bil or bbl (probable -> probabile) (obliga -> obbliga)

Stuff about things

``Determiners'' just means adjectives that have to do with amountsish. The ENDINGS of the adjectives must agree with the noun plurality and gender, except for qualunque and qualsiasi.

A little to a lot

nessuno — none(not one)
niente — none(nothing)
qualunque — any(qualunque and qualsiasi are interchangeable)
chiunque — anybody
qualsiasi — any
qualcuno — someone
qualcosa — something
(or tutti e due)entrambi — both
alcuni — some
poco — little
molti — many
tanti — many
parecchie — many
tutto — everything
tutti — everyone
