Formal Tense (Il Formale) (top)

To speak formally to a person, simply use the 3rd-person feminine instead of the 2nd-person. Yes, instead of speaking to 'you', you are speaking about 'her'.

It goes without saying that all of the normal rules follow: You say 'lei' instead of 'tu'. You say 'la' instead of 'ti' (direct object pronoun), and you say 'le' instead of 'ti' (indirect object pronoun), and so forth.

You may optionally capitalize the 'l' when writing to make it clear you are using the formal and not speaking about a woman.


Conjugating Reflexive Verbs (top)

Reflexive verbs are verbs that one can do unto themselves. An example of a reflexive verb is alzarsi (to get oneself up). In order to conjugate a reflexive verb, you must use these articles:

Reflexive Verb Articles
mici
tivi
sisi

Following the article is the verb conjugated normally. To do this, drop the "si" and add "e"; then treat it as a regular verb. Example:

Sentence: "I get up."
In Italian: "Io alzarsi."
Use reflexive article: "Mi alzarsi."
Conjugate verb normally: "Mi alzo."

For past tense, insert the conjugated form of essere in-between the reflexive article and the verb. Then make the verb past tense. Since you are using essere, remember to agree the last letter of the verb with the subject! Example:

Sentence: "She got up."
In Italian: "Lei alzarsi."
Use reflexive article: "Si alzarsi."
Add conjugated form of essere: "Si è alzarsi."
Conjugate verb in past tense: "Si è alzato."
Agree last letter of verb with subject: "Si è alzata."

Conjugating Reciprical Verbs (top)

Reciprical Verbs are extreemly similar to reflexive verbs. Reciprical verbs are exchanged between two or more people. An example of a reciprical verb is abbraciarsi (to hug eachother). Because reciprical verbs can only happen between two or more people, they will never be seen with singular articles.

Reciprical Verb Articles
ci
vi
si

Present tense example:

Sentence: "We hug eachother."
In Italian: "Noi abbraciarsi."
Use reciprical article: "Ci abbraciarsi."
Conjugate verb normally: "Ci abbraciamo."

For past tense, insert the conjugated form of essere in-between the reflexive article and the verb. Then make the verb past tense. Example:

Sentence: "We hugged eachother."
In Italian: "Noi abbraciarsi."
Use reflexive article: "Ci abbraciarsi."
Add conjugated form of essere: "Ci siamo abbraciarsi."
Conjugate verb in past tense: "Ci siamo abbraciato."

Suffixes (top)

Suffixes (i suffisi) are added to the ends of nouns to describe them. To do so, take off the vowel at the end of a noun and add the appropriate suffix ending.

origional noun ending

smallinoinainiine
small/cuteettoettaettiette
bigoneonaonione
endearmentuccioucciaucciucce
ugly/naughtyaccioacciaacciacce

Note that the masculine singular form for big is one and not ono. Also note that the plural forms of uccio and accio disclude the i after the two c's. Four words become masculine when they use the suffix one, they are: finestra(window), porta(door), palla(ball), and minestra(soup). This means they also use the suffix one instead of ona.


Comparatives (top)

Examples of English comparatives are "I have more ice cream than you," and "I play tennis less than I play soccer." The following is a list of different uses of comparatives in basic sentence structure. The sentences can get more complex than what you see here by adding phrases, adjectives, and so forth. Più is "more" and meno is "less." Di is substituted with che in certain cases.

Comparisons of Equality
(Subject) (avere/essere) cosi (adjective) come (Direct Object).
(S) (avere/essere) tanto (adj/n) quanto (DO).
(S) (v) come/quanto/tanto (DO).
Comparisons of Inequality
(S) (avere/essere) più/meno (adj/n) di (DO).
(S) (avere/essere) più/meno di (adj/n).
(S) (avere/essere) più/meno (adj/n/v in infinitive [are/ire/ere ending]) che (adj/n)
(S) (avere/essere) più/meno a (n) che a (n).


How do we translate "better/best" into Italian? We use "meglio" and "migliore"! But "better" does NOT translate into "meglio", and "best" does NOT traslate into "migliore"!!

migliore = più buono (describes a noun)
meglio = più bene (describes a verb)

The one exception is that "x is better than y" uses "meglio" (regardless of the nounverbiness of x and y).

Examples:

1. The apple is better than the pear. La mela è meglio della pera. (follows exception because of "x _____ di y" construct)

2. The apple is a better choice, with respect to the pear. La mela è una scelta migliore, rispetto alla pera. (no exception because the grammatical construct didn't fit)

2. This is the best apple I've ever had. È la migliore mela che ho mai assaggiato.

3. He runs better than her. Corre meglio che lei.

4. It's best to go to bed. È meglio andare a letto.


The Imperfect Tense (top)

The imperfect tense is used generally for past habitual actions, meaning something you did over a period of time in the past. Example: "I went to school every monday" would use the imperfect. Imperfect is also used when talking about time, physical states, mental states, and emotional states.

To use the tense, take off the "re" at the end of a verb and add the following endings...

vovamo
vivate
vavano

1) Appetizers - L'antipasto
2) First Course - Il Primo (pasta/carbs)

3) Side Dish - Il Contorno (ordered with second course)
